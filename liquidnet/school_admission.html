Admission Process & Selection

The school does not use a typical open-enrollment application. Instead, it follows a targeted recruitment process focused on vulnerability and potential.

District-Based Recruitment

Youth are selected from each of the 30 Rwandan districts.

Selection Criteria

The school prioritizes the "most vulnerable" students, including orphans, those living in severe poverty, or those with refugee status.

Gender Balance

To support girls' education, the village maintains a ratio of approximately 60% female and 40% male students.

Internal Capability

While focusing on vulnerability, selectors also look for students who have the "internal capabilities" to thrive in the village's intensive educational and healing environment.

Academic Requirements & Structure

  • Entry Level: Students typically enter after completing middle school (Senior Level 3).
  • Enrichment Year: The first year is a specialized "catch-up" or enrichment period designed to prepare students for the rigors of high school.
  • Academic Combinations: Following the enrichment year, students choose a "combination" (similar to a major), such as History, Economics, and Geography (HEG), which they study for the next three years.

A Message from Our Principal

Dr. Jean Paul

School Principal

April 9, 2026

Dear Prospective Families and Guardians,

Welcome to Liquidnet Family High School, where we believe that every child deserves the opportunity to heal, grow, and succeed. As the academic heart of the Agahozo-Shalom Youth Village, our school is not just an educational institution—it's a sanctuary for Rwanda's most vulnerable youth.

Since our founding in 2008, we have maintained a 100% pass rate on national exams and sent 65% of our graduates to universities. But our success is measured not just in academic achievements, but in the lives transformed through our unique approach that combines rigorous education with emotional healing.

Our admissions process is designed to identify students who will thrive in our supportive community. We look for young people with the internal capabilities to benefit from our intensive program, while prioritizing those facing the greatest challenges—orphans, children from extreme poverty, and refugees.

If you are considering LFHS for your child or ward, please contact us directly. Our team is here to guide you through the process and answer any questions you may have.

With hope and determination,
Dr. Jean Paul

Academic Programs at LFHS

Our comprehensive curriculum combines academic excellence with practical skills and character development.

Enrichment Year (EY)

A dedicated "catch-up" year focused on English language acquisition, IT skills, and emotional healing before advanced academics begin.

  • English language immersion
  • Basic computer skills
  • Emotional support and counseling
  • Foundation mathematics and science

Science & Technology (STEAM)

Robotics, coding, 3D design, and innovation lab where students prototype real-world solutions for Rwanda's challenges.

  • Robotics and automation
  • Programming and app development
  • 3D printing and design
  • Environmental science projects

Media & Journalism

The Liquidnet Student Magazine, professional-grade film production, and photography studio where students tell powerful stories.

  • Digital journalism
  • Film production
  • Photography
  • Public speaking

Leadership & Clubs

Over 20 student clubs including Debate, Robotics, Journalism, Arts, Athletics. Plus Village Time—our signature Friday talent show.

  • Student government
  • Debate and public speaking
  • Community service
  • Cultural performances

Academic Combinations

Students choose specialized combinations like History, Economics, and Geography (HEG) or Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM).

  • HEG: Humanities focus
  • PCM: Science focus
  • MCB: Biology focus
  • Flexible pathways

Holistic Development

Integration of Tikkun Ha'lev (healing the heart) and Tikkun Olam (repairing the world) through counseling, mentorship, and service.

  • Individual counseling
  • Group therapy sessions
  • Mentorship programs
  • Community impact projects
